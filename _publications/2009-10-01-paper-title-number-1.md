excerpt: 'Detecting defects in fabrics is challenging due to the numerous variations in fabric types and defects. Traditional methods have struggled with low detection rates and accuracy. To address this, we implemented multilevel modeling for enhanced performance. Our approach compares advanced deep learning models—MobileNetV2, Xception, VGG19, and InceptionV3—evaluating their performance across different fabric types. Initially, a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) classifies the fabric with 97.6% accuracy, followed by defect detection using the optimal model for each fabric type. This method significantly improves overall defect detection performance. Additionally, K-fold cross-validation ensures the robustness of the proposed model.'
